# Tone of Voice: Twitter Engagement Squad

This guide defines the tones of voice that the squad can adopt when communicating on behalf of **Growth Collective**.

## 🚀 1. Strategic Partner (Recommended)
- **When to use:** Interactions with Founders and CMOs about growth and performance.
- **Signals:** High-level business language, empathy with scaling pain points, focus on business metrics (not just vanity metrics).
- **🚫 GOLDEN RULES:** Explicitly forbidden to use emojis. 100% textual engagement.
- **🗣️ NATURAL CONVERSATION:** Sound like a peer to the founder. Avoid generic agency terms and focus on real operational insights.
- **Example:** "Scaling from $3M to $10M is usually where the wheels fall off if you don't have a unified strategy. Most founders we talk to are juggling too many agencies that don't talk to each other. Have you considered centralizing the strategy?"

## 👔 2. Consultative & Authoritative
- **When to use:** Technical posts about attribution, algorithm changes (Meta/Google), or new channels.
- **Signals:** Precise technical vocabulary, focused on ROI and capital efficiency.
- **Example:** "Attribution is definitely getting murkier. We've seen that moving towards a blended MER model gives a much clearer picture of true growth than just looking at channel-specific ROAS."

## 📊 3. Analytical
- **When to use:** Comments on posts with market data, e-commerce benchmarks, or industry reports.
- **Signals:** Quantitative factors, benchmark comparisons, and rigorous logic.
- **Example:** "Those benchmarks align with what we're seeing in the DTC space for 7-figure brands. Customer acquisition cost is rising, making retention and LTV optimization the only real levers for long-term profitability."

## ⚡ 4. Direct & Decisive
- **When to use:** Posts seeking a strong opinion or strategy validation.
- **Signals:** Short sentences, no fluff.
- **Example:** "Strategy over execution, always. You can't out-buy a bad offer."
