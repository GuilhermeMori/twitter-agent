# Research Brief: Social Media Metrics Analysis

## Core Frameworks
1. **The Social Media Funnel**:
    - **Awareness**: Reach, Impressions, Follower Growth.
    - **Engagement**: Engagement Rate (ER), Likes, Comments, Shares, Saves.
    - **Conversion**: CTR (Click-Through Rate), Site Visits, Conversions.
2. **Goal-to-Metric Mapping**: Every tracked metric must align with a specific business goal (e.g., "Grow Brand" -> Follower Growth).

## Output Examples (High Quality)
- **Executive Summary**: 3 top-line wins/losses, current status vs. goals.
- **Platform Breakdown**: Specific tables for Twitter, LinkedIn, and Instagram.
- **Comparison Table**: Current Period vs. Previous Period vs. Baseline.
- **Actionable Insights**: Narrative explaining the "Why" and the "So What".

## Anti-Patterns (Avoid)
- **Vanity Metrics obsession**: Reporting high impressions without engagement or conversions.
- **Lack of Context**: Comparing a "holiday week" to a "normal week" without noting the difference.
- **Fragmented Data**: Not unifying metrics across platforms for a holistic view.
- **Passive Reporting**: Stating numbers without recommendations.

## Voice Guidance (Vocabulary)
- **Always use**: "Period-over-period growth", "Engagement velocity", "Delta", "Baseline", "Conversion funnel".
- **Never use**: "Doing well", "A lot of likes", "People seem to enjoy it".

## Quality Criteria
- Data accuracy (verified against source).
- Clear attribution of growth drivers (e.g., "Viral post X drove 40% of new followers").
- Actionable next steps (e.g., "Post more video content on Tuesday").
